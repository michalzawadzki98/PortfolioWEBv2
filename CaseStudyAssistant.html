üí¢ Research & Problem Statement

Customers purchasing beauty products online face the challenge of finding the right product that meets their specific needs. The vast array of available products can be overwhelming for buyers, and the product selection process takes a considerable amount of time. Customers buying beauty products often lack sufficient knowledge about conscious skincare, making it difficult for them to choose the right product.

The issue of dissatisfaction and difficulty in finding the desired product ranks among the top 5 most commonly mentioned problems that consumers encounter during online shopping.

The presence of a wide range of products beyond cosmetics on the largest marketplace in Poland further complicates the beauty product shopping experience, leading consumers to prefer other platforms. All of this contributes to the fact that shopping for beauty products on the largest marketplace in Poland is not very popular among consumers. This, in turn, results in low sales for the official L'Or√©al store on that platform.

üí° Generating Ideas & Selection

Taking into account the business requirements and the problems defined based on research and user interviews, we proceeded to generate ideas.

We evaluated the best ideas based on their uniqueness and how well they addressed real user problems.

We concluded that the best idea was to create an intelligent cosmetics selection assistant. Through asking relevant questions and leveraging modern technologies such as skin scanning based on photos, it would tailor the perfect product for the consumer's needs. Consequently, it would guide them to the official L'Or√©al store on the largest marketplace in Poland, where the consumer could purchase the recommended product.

The assistant was envisioned as a web application, accessible by navigating to a dedicated website, for example, through a link. The assistant could be integrated directly with the marketplace website. It would not require the installation of an application or the registration of an account.

üéÆ Prototyping & Testing

After selecting and discussing the idea in terms of the functionalities it should include, I created user interface mockups and an interactive prototype in Figma.

Since the assistant is a simple application and I can quickly create high-fidelity mockups, I decided to skip low-fidelity ones. This streamlines testing and helps users better understand the prototype. The mockups are styled to match the desired marketplace service for potential direct integration.

Testing with users

We conducted prototype tests with 3 beauty product consumers and 3 beauty/e-commerce industry experts.

Each user launched the prototype on their device, sharing their screen during testing.

Key notes regarding potential improvements:

  • Lack of an easy transition to searching for the next product.
  • We should display complementary products, not just a single item.
  • A brief description of the matched product is missing on the product screen.
  • We should provide users with the option to use an advanced and quick assistant form.
